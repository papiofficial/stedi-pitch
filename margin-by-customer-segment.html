Blended ARR hides where the real money lives.

A Workers' Comp-heavy customer, an eligibility-heavy GenAI startup, and a late-stage health system with 278 prior auth volume do not make the same kind of business. Their contribution margins diverge by 2-3x once you layer in support hours, enrollment ops load, and payer pass-through. This tool segments Stedi's customer base into five archetypes and computes per-archetype margin the way BizOps would want it: live, adjustable, auditable.

What this is: The margin calculator from opportunity #5 in the Strategic Teardown, built as a working tool. Everything is adjustable. Every number cites a source in the "Show my work" panel.

    Revenue model

    • Per-transaction rates from stedi.com/pricing: eligibility $0.15, claims (837) $0.20, ERA (835) $0.15/paid claim, prior auth $0.20 est, claim status $0.15 (included), Workers' Comp claims $0.25. First 100/type/month free.
    • Volume discount applied on top of list rates. Defaults loosely calibrated from Enterprise tier notes.
    • Platform floor: $500/mo Developer minimum. Applied to archetypes below the floor.

    Cost model

    • AWS compute + storage: $0.002/transaction benchmark. Per-tenant AWS account isolation is confirmed (Stedi blog). At scale this compresses toward $0.001.
    • Support labor: hours × $100/hour fully-loaded cost. Stedi runs a <10 min median response across 1,500+ dedicated customer Slack channels (Series C). Heavy-touch archetypes consume disproportionate time.
    • Enrollment ops load: $5/active enrollment record/month. Tracks every payer × provider × transaction-type combo that needs periodic recertification. Enrollment paperwork errors drive ~24% of denials industry-wide (Optum 2024 Denials Index).
    • Payer pass-through fees: billed at cost per Stedi's pricing page. Revenue- and cost-neutral, but reduces gross margin %.

    Archetypes

    • Eligibility-Heavy Startup: 10K/mo, 70% eligibility / 20% claims / 10% ERA. Low support (2 hrs/mo), 20 active enrollments. The margin trap: sits at the $500/mo floor, 15-30% margin after AWS.
    • Mixed Mid-Market: 500K/mo, balanced mix. 6 hrs support, 100 enrollments. The sweet spot: $70-90K revenue, 50-60% margin.
    • Enterprise Platform: 10M/mo, mixed incl. prior auth. 20 hrs support, 500 enrollments, custom integration cost. Scale margin but heavy payer pass-through.
    • Workers' Comp Niche: 100K/mo, 80% WC claims at $0.25, niche payer set. High support (12 hrs) because WC payers are fussy. Higher per-txn revenue, but denser payer complexity.
    • Prior-Auth Heavy: 50K/mo, 30% 278. Specialty health (cardio, oncology). High support (15 hrs) because 278 is ~32% electronically adopted industry-wide per CAQH; fallback to portal/peer-to-peer calls.

    What's not modeled

    • Acquisition cost (CAC) is not in this unit-economics view. Add it for LTV/CAC.
    • Fixed R&D overhead for payer connectivity maintenance (rough estimate: 60-70% of Stedi R&D spend per industry norm). Allocated separately at the company level, not per archetype.
    • Compliance overhead (SOC 2, HIPAA BAA, audits) is a fixed cost, not variable per archetype.
    • Network effects: new payer connections reduce marginal onboarding cost for every customer. Positive externality not captured here.

    How I'd harden this for production

    • Replace preset archetype assumptions with live queries against the data warehouse. Every active customer gets segmented automatically based on actual usage mix, support hours, and enrollment load.
    • Surface the per-archetype margin in the FP&A dashboard side-by-side with CAC, LTV, and retention cohort data. That is opportunity #1 from the Strategic Teardown.
    • Tie to pricing decisions: when list prices drop (Kanter's tenet "prices never go up, only down"), the model re-runs and shows which archetypes remain healthy and which compress.
    • Tie to sales comp: a portion of commission should reflect archetype fit. A referenceable mid-market logo is worth more than a thin-margin startup, and the comp plan should say so.
    • Close the loop with the Clearinghouse Cost Comparator: every closed deal writes its archetype fingerprint back to this model to keep the assumptions calibrated.

    Why this matters at Stedi specifically

    • 1/3 of the customer base is native GenAI companies (Series B). That cohort's usage pattern is not the long-term mix.
    • 6x paying customers, 7x billed transactions YoY (Series C). Revenue per customer is moving. Blended ARR hides which tiers are compounding.
    • Pricing, headcount, and investment decisions made on blended margin will systematically misallocate investment. That is the forecasting hazard BizOps should be protecting Finance from.
